About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor, where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Mingyan Liu. My research focuses on the intersection of game theory and machine learning, specifically aiming to improve the robustness of algorithmic decision-making systems against strategic manipulation. I am fortunate to be supported by an NSF graduate research fellowship.

Before coming to Michigan, I received my B.S. in Electrical Engineering from George Mason University. I have also spent time doing research at MIT, working on Bayesian filtering in chaotic systems.

Research Interests

Stochastic Control • Game Theory • Reinforcement Learning • Machine Learning • Cybersecurity
